# AI Outreach Prompt Templates

Ready-to-use prompts for Claude, ChatGPT, or any LLM. Copy, paste, and fill in the bracketed placeholders. All prompts are designed to keep your outreach specific to your account strategy—not generic.

---

## 1. Account Research & Signal Monitoring (Weekly)

**When:** Start of each week  
**Tool:** Claude or ChatGPT (with web browsing if available)

```
Summarize the last 7 days of news for [Ciena / Sagent / U.S. Financial Technology]: executive moves, earnings, product launches, competitive mentions. Output 3 bullet points and 1 suggested outreach angle that ties to my first workload.
```

**For Ciena:** Add — *"Focus on CFO, supply chain, AI demand, or backlog."*  
**For Sagent:** Add — *"Focus on Dara, CEO/President, deployment rollouts, or board/outcomes."*  
**For US FinTech:** Add — *"Focus on platform pivot, securitization, FHFA, or data monetization."*

---

## 2. Personalized Outreach Email

**When:** Before sending any cold or warm outreach  
**Input:** Account context + your POV + recent news

```
Write a 3-sentence email to [role, e.g., VP FP&A] at [company]. Reference [recent event, e.g., Q3 earnings / new CFO] and tie to [specific workload from my strategy]. Tone: professional, specific, no fluff. Do not use "I hope this finds you well" or similar.
```

**Example filled:**
```
Write a 3-sentence email to VP FP&A at Ciena. Reference their raised FY26 guidance and new CFO. Tie to backlog risk and margin visibility on AI deals—I can show risk on 2-3 deals in 24 hours. Tone: professional, specific, no fluff.
```

## 3. Discovery Call Prep (Pre-Call)

**When:** 30 minutes before a discovery call  
**Input:** Prospect's LinkedIn or role + your hypothesis

```
Generate 5 discovery questions that validate or invalidate this hypothesis: "[paste hypothesis from deck]"

For context: I'm meeting with [role] at [company]. My first workload is [paste from deck]. I need to understand: do they have this problem, who owns it, and what happens if they don't fix it?
```

**Example for Ciena:**
```
Generate 5 discovery questions that validate or invalidate: "Ciena's risk is not demand. It's execution against that demand."

For context: I'm meeting with FP&A lead at Ciena. My first workload is backlog risk + margin visibility on AI deals. I need to understand: do they have this problem, who owns it, and what happens if they don't fix it?
```

## 4. Discovery Call Debrief (Post-Call)

**When:** Right after a call  
**Input:** Raw meeting notes (paste as-is)

```
Extract from these meeting notes:
1. Confirmed pain (what did they say is broken?)
2. Objections or concerns raised
3. Next steps and owner
4. Who else should be involved

Meeting notes:
[paste notes]
```

## 5. Competitive Battle Card (Databricks / Others)

**When:** Competitor comes up in conversation or you need quick intel

```
Snowflake vs Databricks for [workload type, e.g., operational analytics in regulated financial services]. Key differentiators in 3 bullets. Emphasize: governed data, no new pipelines, operational layer, speed to value.
```

---

## 6. Executive Briefing One-Pager

**When:** Before an executive meeting  
**Input:** Company name + your deck context

```
Create a 1-page executive briefing for [company]. Include:
- Company snapshot (1 line)
- Snowflake today (1 line)
- Proposed first workload (1 line)
- Proof point we can deliver (1 line)
- Why now / compelling event (1 line)

Use this strategy: [paste account section from deck]
```
